## Competitive Positioning

### Rize's Key Differentiators
1. **Zero-friction** — no timers to start/stop, fully automatic
2. **Privacy-first** — no screenshots, no keyloggers, employee controls data
3. **AI categorization** — ML-driven client/project assignment
4. **Desktop-native** — deep OS integration for accurate tracking
5. **Profitability focus** — not just time tracking but margin analysis

### Rize's Weaknesses (Tabatha Opportunities)
1. **Desktop-only** — no browser extension, misses browser-specific context
2. **No tab management** — pure time tracking, no browsing workflow tools
3. **No intent/context system** — tracks WHAT you did, not WHY
4. **No "Good Friction"** — no gatekeeping against mindless browsing
5. **No session/workspace management** — can't save/restore browsing contexts
6. **No real-time tab organization** — no groups, sub-groups, priorities on tabs
7. **Expensive** — $10-20/mo vs. free extension
8. **No URL locking** — can't jail a tab to a domain
9. **No step-away mode** — no proactive pause/resume workflow
