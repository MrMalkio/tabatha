> "Maybe it just uses Google-Meet-type mechanics — or leverages what people set up themselves: drop their Meet link, their Twitch, their Kick, their YouTube Live — and they could peek into others while streaming on one and viewing on another, but see it in one experience."

## What It Does

Pairs Tabatha users who are live and working into lightweight co-working sessions
(the body-doubling accountability effect), via a queue + one-click match. BYO-video
first: users drop their own Meet/Twitch/Kick/YouTube-Live link; Tabatha provides the
pairing, presence, and one-experience frame — not the video infrastructure.

## First scoping pass (when picked up)

1. **Research task first:** the science of body doubling (what makes it work —
   presence, mild observation, parallel intent) → which mechanics to replicate
   digitally (the digital version is #182's Chaperone).
2. MVP: presence queue ("body-doubling: available") on the account layer →
   match → exchange BYO link → both users' Context Views show the pairing.
3. Later: embedded viewing, multi-peek, streaming-service integrations.
