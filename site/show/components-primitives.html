Component Showcase

Primitives & Panels

The building blocks under every Tabatha surface: buttons and glass cards, the autocomplete and tagging inputs that bind a focus to its hierarchy, and the full-panel overlays for commands, shortcuts, tasks, and timelines. Every string, colour, and radius here is lifted from the shipping source, including the places where the product hardcodes a value off the scale.

Component library

14 primitives and panels · rendered from live app tokens

The shared vocabulary. Every surface in Tabatha is assembled from these, which is why the product feels consistent without a component framework doing the enforcing - there is no design-system dependency here, just a small set of pieces used everywhere. GlassCard, PopButton and the pickers carry the Pop-Art palette and the halved 2/4/8 radius scale. The global overlays are also here: the command palette, the fourteen keyboard shortcuts, and the link/merge modal that resolves the duplicate contexts you inevitably create.

TagPicker
Binds a focus to Realm › Client › Project › Task. Collapsed it is a dashed affordance that becomes a breadcrumb once filled; expanded it exposes the realm toggle and the ComboInputs, with autofill up the hierarchy. Switching to Personal auto-fills the client as Self (always offered as an option, whatever your client list holds) and drops Client and Project from the form unless they already carry a value. Task stays available in both realms.
