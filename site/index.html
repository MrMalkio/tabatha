What it actually believes

Context over chaos
No tab exists in a vacuum. Every one belongs to something you are actually doing, and it inherits that the moment you open it.
Intentional browsing
Friction is good when it stops you mid-drift. Tabatha asks the question, then gets out of the way. You still choose the distraction. You just choose it on purpose.
Universal time
Time tracking should not be a second app you forget to start. It belongs in the browser, running whether or not you remembered.
Agent-ready data
Your context, intent and attention become structured data. An agent can read what you are working on instead of guessing — and help you do better work with surgical accuracy.
